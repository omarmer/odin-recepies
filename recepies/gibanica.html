Gibanica

Gibanica, a traditional Serbian pastry, is a beloved culinary treasure renowned for its rich, savory layers and comforting taste. This multi-layered pie, often a centerpiece at celebrations, seamlessly blends the crispiness of phyllo dough with the creamy depth of cheese, creating a harmonious fusion of textures and flavors.

The recipe for Gibanica begins with thin sheets of phyllo dough, which are lightly brushed with oil or melted butter to achieve a golden, flaky crust upon baking. The filling, a hearty mixture of eggs and cheese, typically combines crumbled feta for its tangy bite and smooth cottage cheese for a creamy consistency. Some variations also include spinach or minced meat, catering to diverse palates. The filling is generously spread between the phyllo layers, often with a sprinkle of aromatic herbs like dill or parsley for an added burst of flavor. Once assembled, the pie is baked to perfection, resulting in a golden-brown exterior that encases a moist, rich interior. This delightful dish is not only a staple at Serbian gatherings but has also gained popularity in neighboring Balkan countries, celebrated for its comforting taste and the joy it brings to family tables.

  • Ingredients:
    • Phyllo dough sheets
    • Crumbled feta cheese
    • Cottage cheese
    • Eggs
    • Vegetable oil or melted butter
    • Optional: spinach or minced meat
    • Optional: herbs like dill or parsley
  • Instructions:
    • Preheat oven to 180°C (350°F).
    • Brush each phyllo dough sheet lightly with oil or melted butter.
    • In a bowl, mix the eggs, feta cheese, and cottage cheese.
    • Add optional spinach or minced meat and herbs if desired.
    • Layer the phyllo sheets in a baking dish, adding the cheese mixture between layers.
    • Bake until the pastry is golden brown, about 30-45 minutes.
    • Allow to cool slightly before serving.
